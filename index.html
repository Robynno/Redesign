The Margaret E. Heggan Library's Website

The Margaret E. Heggan library is a public library serving the residents of Washington Township New Jersey. The library offers a variety of services to the community including books, movies, and computer access. The library also offers a variety of programs for children and adults. Their website serves as the primary platform for showcasing library resources, programs, and services.

The library's website currently serves as a hub for accessing digital resources, browsing the catalog, reserving books, checking event schedules, and engaging with community programs. Therefore it is imperative that the Library's websites remains accessible and allows for easy navigation for users of all ages. The purpose of this redesign is to do just that!

Current Issues With The Page

Accessibility

The website has significant accessibility issues, with 31 errors and 207 alerts identified by WebAIM. The most critical problems include missing alternative text for images (24 instances), unlabeled form elements, empty links, and contrast errors. Additionally, there are numerous labeling issues, redundant links, and overly long alternative text, all of which impact usability for individuals relying on assistive technologies. Addressing these issues will improve accessibility and ensure a more inclusive user experience.

Usability

The website's efficiency is hindered by an overwhelming number of tabs and scattered information, making navigation complex. In terms of learnability, the disorganized layout requires users to scroll and search extensively to locate information. Memorability is rated 5/10, with the right-hand side being the most recognizable element due to its consistent display of location and library placement. Improving structure and organization would enhance the usability and user experience.

Redesign

The redesign aimed to transform the library website into a more navigable, user-friendly portal. By streamlining navigation, using clear and consistent visual cues (like full-width red bars, intuitive carousels, and centered content), and implementing responsive design techniques, the new layout makes it easier for users to quickly access library hours, events, news, and featured book collections. It also simplifies the navigation of the website by removing unneccasry imagery and words and streamline navigation with a hamburgers or toggles
